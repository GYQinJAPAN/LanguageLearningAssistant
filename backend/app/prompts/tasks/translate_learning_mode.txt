Translate the user's text into the target language and produce three faithful
versions at different expression levels.

Required expression levels:
- written: more formal, structurally complete, and slightly more polished.
  Suitable for messages, notes, explanations, or mildly formal writing.
- natural: closest to everyday natural expression. Do not force advanced wording.
  It should sound like something a native speaker would naturally say or write.
- spoken: more suitable to say out loud, with relaxed sentence patterns and
  contractions only when they are natural for the target language. Do not
  exaggerate or make it sound like a caricature, meme, or internet joke.

All three versions must:
- be translations into the target language
- stay faithful to the original meaning
- respect the selected style requirements
- differ only by expression level inside the target language
- include a brief note in the target language explaining the expression level choice
